# Japanese Grammar - Beginner Level

## Sentence Structure

Japanese uses **Subject - Object - Verb (SOV)** structure.

- English: I eat an apple. (S-V-O)
- Japanese: I apple eat. (S-O-V) -> _Watashi wa ringo o tabemasu._

## Particles (Josh)

Particles are small suffixes that indicate the function of a word in a sentence.

### Wa (は) - Topic Marker

Marks the topic of the sentence. Pronounced "wa" but written as "ha".

- _Watashi **wa** Tanaka desu._ (As for me, I am Tanaka.)

### Ga (が) - Subject Marker

Marks the grammatical subject. Often used with specific verbs (likes/dislikes, ability) or for new information.

- _Neko **ga** imasu._ (There is a cat.)
- _Watashi wa sushi **ga** suki desu._ (I like sushi.)

### O (を) - Object Marker

Marks the direct object of an action. Pronounced "o" (sometimes "wo").

- _Mizu **o** nomimasu._ (Drink water.)

### Ni (に) - Target/Time/Location Marker

Indicates movement toward a place, a specific time, or location of existence.

- _Gakkou **ni** ikimasu._ (Go **to** school.)
- _Rokuji **ni** okimasu._ (Wake up **at** 6 o'clock.)

### De (で) - Context/Means Marker

Indicates where an action takes place or by what means.

- _Basu **de** ikimasu._ (Go **by** bus.)
- _Resutoran **de** tabemasu._ (Eat **at** a restaurant.)

### No (の) - Possession Marker

Indicates possession or modifies nouns. Works like "'s" in English.

- _Watashi **no** hon._ (My book.)

### Ka (か) - Question Marker

Added to the end of a sentence to turn it into a question.

- _Genki desu **ka**?_ (Are you well?)

## Basic Verb Conjugation

### Desu (To be)

- Present Affirmative: **Desu** (It is)
- Present Negative: **Ja arimasen** (It is not)
- Past Affirmative: **Deshita** (It was)
- Past Negative: **Ja arimasen deshita** (It was not)

### Masu Form (Polite Verbs)

- Present Affirmative: **-masu** (Tabemasu - eat)
- Present Negative: **-masen** (Tabemasen - don't eat)
- Past Affirmative: **-mashita** (Tabemashita - ate)
- Past Negative: **-masen deshita** (Tabemasen deshita - didn't eat)
